Each place on this map holds people whose daily lives are shaped by forces most of us never see — the water that runs short, the minerals beneath the land, the armed men at the checkpoint, the supply chain that connects their loss to our comfort. These briefings name those connections so that when we pray, we pray with specificity.

These briefings draw on data from 45 sources — UN agencies, human rights organisations, environmental monitors, and community voices — to trace how resource extraction, conflict, displacement, and ecological breakdown are connected in specific places. The prayer structure follows the Anglican Common Worship tradition: psalm, collect, and intercessions grounded in the material reality of each place.
