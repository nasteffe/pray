What Is Happening

In Sudan, over 21.2M people do not know where their next meal will come from. Water withdrawal exceeds what nature can replenish — families queue for hours or walk kilometers for drinking water. 10.1M people have been forced from their homes and are living in temporary shelters, with host families, or in the open.

Intercessions

home and family

We hold before God our homes, families, friends and all whom we love — especially the 21,241,056 people facing hunger in Sudan, and communities in Khartoum, North Kordofan enduring the worst conditions.

the displaced

that the 10.1M internally displaced and 2.5M refugees — many fleeing Khartoum and North Kordofan — may find safety, shelter, and the hope of return.

those facing famine

for the 375,389 people in famine conditions, especially in Al buram, Delami, IDPs/Refugees Abasiya, A.R.A Shargi, Diling & Kadugli, that food may reach them urgently.

water and creation

for communities facing critical water stress (119% withdrawal rate), that they may have clean water for drinking and growing food.

health workers and the sick

for the 46 health workers killed bringing care to the wounded and 25 health facilities damaged or destroyed — that healing may not be silenced by violence.

the people of Aj Jazirah

for those enduring the worst of this crisis — 4,865,972 facing food crisis in Aj Jazirah alone.

peace and justice

that leaders may choose dialogue over violence, and that the rule of law may be restored.

For Reflection

If neither the Rapid Support Forces nor the Sudanese Armed Forces can achieve legitimacy through violence, what forms of authority are communities in Sudan already recognizing — and how might we support those?

Neither side can achieve legitimacy through violence. The question is whether communities already building alternative governance structures — local peace committees, emergency response rooms, women's networks — represent the legitimacy both sides claim to seek.
