For Prayer

Last updated on 2026-07-20.

For Haiti…

For Haiti above all — for Port-au-Prince held by armed gangs, its hospitals and schools shut, its people penned into neighbourhoods by checkpoints and fire. For the hundreds of thousands driven from their homes within the city, and for the women and girls on whom the violence falls hardest.

For the guns and the hunger…

For a nation where the guns arrive from abroad and the food does not, where hunger has reached the edge of famine in a country that fed itself within living memory. That the flow of weapons is stopped, and the flow of help is not.

For the storm-exposed…

For the island nations in the path of ever-stronger hurricanes — Dominica and the Bahamas rebuilding yet again, the low coasts and the fishing villages — who did least to warm the ocean now rising against them.

For those who leave and those who send…

For the Caribbean's great diaspora, holding families and islands together with what they send home; and for those who cross to the Dominican Republic or take to open boats, and are so often turned back.

For the long inheritance…

For islands still carrying the afterlife of slavery and empire in their debts, their soils, and their divided wealth. For reparative justice, and for the dignity of peoples who made beauty of what was done to them.

For those who hold on…

For the Haitian nurses and teachers and pastors who have not left, the neighbourhood brigades, the market women who still open their stalls. That their refusal to abandon their country is honoured, and helped.

Investigates illicit financial flows, corruption, and natural resource exploitation. Has documented how Caribbean offshore financial centres launder proceeds from extractive industries and arms trafficking, and how climate finance fails to reach frontline communities.

Traces the financial architecture the briefing identifies — where offshore banking in the Cayman Islands and British Virgin Islands launders proceeds from drug and weapons trafficking that fuel violence in Haiti and Jamaica, while the same jurisdictions shelter extractive industry profits from taxation.

Caribbean Natural Resources Institute (CANARI) solidarity

Regional technical institute supporting community-based natural resource management across the Caribbean. Works with fisherfolk organizations, forest-dependent communities, and coastal communities on participatory governance of shared resources.

Oxfam — Haiti solidarity

Provides humanitarian assistance and supports community resilience in Haiti, the Caribbean's most crisis-affected state. Works on food security, water access, and disaster preparedness in contexts of armed group control and institutional collapse.

Operates at the sharpest point of Caribbean crisis the briefing documents — where armed groups control Port-au-Prince's fuel and food distribution, charcoal deforestation accelerates flood vulnerability, and remittance-dependent households lose access to basic services as state institutions disintegrate.

AIDA (Interamerican Association for Environmental Defense) advocacy

Uses law to protect the environment and communities affected by extractive and development projects across Latin America and the Caribbean. Has brought cases on coastal development, reef destruction, and mining in Caribbean island nations.
