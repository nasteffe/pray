For Ukraine at war…

For the people of Ukraine, deep into a full-scale war with no end in sight — the cities under missiles and drones, the front-line towns erased, the families divided across borders, and the young men on both sides who will not come home. For a just peace, and for strength to endure until it comes.

For the occupied and the taken…

For those living under occupation, and above all for the Ukrainian children taken from their families and carried away — that they are found, returned, and made whole.

For the displaced and those who took them in…

For the millions of Ukrainians scattered across this region and beyond, and for the Poles, Moldovans, and Romanians who opened their homes to them. That welcome does not grow tired.

For the small nations in the shadow…

For Moldova and the small countries of this steppe living in the war's long shadow, and for the wounds of the Balkans just to the south, not yet fully healed.

For the bread-basket…

For the farmers of this great plain — Ukrainian, Polish, Romanian alike — caught between war, blockade, and a market that pays them too little for the grain that feeds distant tables.

For those who keep the lights on…

For the volunteers, the medics, the rail and power crews who keep Ukraine alive under fire. That their endurance is honoured, and not abandoned.
