Who Sustains This

M23 Rebel Group · coltan/tantalum taxation; Rwandan military support; mineral smuggling via Kigali

Driven by: We must protect our community from genocide and exclusion

The trap: Seeking inclusion through rebellion that ensures exclusion. M23 claims to fight for Tutsi rights within Congo, but its Rwandan backing and military approach make political inclusion impossible. The more territory M23 takes, the more Congolese society views Tutsi as foreign agents.

Armed Forces of the DRC · state budget; mining concessions; informal taxation in eastern DRC

Driven by: We must expel foreign-backed aggressors and assert Congolese authority

The trap: Asserting sovereignty through a war that reveals its absence. Decades of campaigns against armed groups have not established state control in the east. The FARDC's weakness and corruption mean each 'victory' is temporary, while the war economy benefits those who profit from instability.

Coalition of Patriots for Change · diamond/gold mining; cattle taxation; cross-border trade

Driven by: We must end the marginalization of the Muslim north

The trap: Fighting marginalization through actions that deepen it. The CPC's armed campaign against Bangui reinforces the very Muslim-vs-Christian narrative that marginalizes northern communities. Each offensive generates anti-Balaka retaliation, entrenching the sectarian divide the CPC claims to oppose.

Russian Instructors / Africa Corps · mining concessions (gold, diamonds); timber concessions; Russian state funding

Driven by: We provide security that France and the UN cannot

The trap: Providing security through methods that create insecurity. Russian forces are documented committing atrocities against civilians they were deployed to protect, while mining operations extract wealth from communities that see no benefit. The security they offer is indistinguishable from another form of exploitation.

For Prayer

Last updated on 2026-07-20.

For the displaced of the east...

For the millions driven from their homes in the eastern Congo as armies and militias fight over the richest ground on earth — around Goma, across North and South Kivu, in Ituri. For families in camps within sight of the hills their villages stood on. That the guns fall quiet long enough for them to go home.

For those who dig the metals of our devices...

For the children and young men digging cobalt and coltan by hand in the Congo's south and east — the metals inside every phone and battery in the world — for a dollar or two a day, in tunnels that collapse. That the world which carries their labour in its pocket learns to see them.

For the forest peoples...

For the Indigenous and forest-dwelling peoples of the Congo Basin, the second lung of the earth, watching the logging roads push in; for those who have kept the forest for a hundred generations and are now called trespassers in it.

For the Central African Republic...

For a country whose war few beyond its borders can name, where cattle raids and diamond concessions fund the fighting; for farmers and herders set against each other, and for families sheltering in churches and in the bush.

For the women of the Kivus...

For the women of the eastern Congo, who have carried this war in their own bodies through decades of violence used as a weapon, and who still hold their families and their markets together. For justice long deferred, and for healing.

For those who document and rebuild...

For the Congolese doctors, the local human-rights monitors, the parish networks and women's cooperatives who record what is done and repair what is broken. That their courage is not spent in vain.

Organizations working at the nodes this briefing traces

IPIS (International Peace Information Service) investigation

Belgian research institute mapping artisanal mining sites, armed group presence, and mineral supply chains in eastern DRC. Produces the most detailed georeferenced data on 3T (tin, tantalum, tungsten) and gold mining across North and South Kivu.

Maps the exact mining sites the briefing traces — artisanal cobalt and coltan operations in Lualaba and North Kivu where armed groups tax production, child labor persists despite due diligence schemes, and minerals enter supply chains destined for electronics manufacturers and EV battery producers.

Global Witness investigation

Investigates the links between natural resource exploitation, conflict, and corruption in the Congo Basin. Published landmark reports on conflict minerals, illegal logging concessions, and oil block allocation in DRC.

Traces the resource-conflict nexus the briefing documents — how cobalt and coltan revenues finance armed groups in the Kivus, how logging concessions overlap with Indigenous Pygmy territories, and how oil exploration in Virunga National Park threatens both gorilla habitat and community fisheries on Lake Edward.

Rainforest Foundation UK solidarity

Supports forest-dependent Indigenous and local communities in the Congo Basin in securing land tenure and resisting industrial logging, mining, and plantation expansion. Works with Bayaka and Batwa Pygmy communities on community mapping and legal rights.

Works with the communities most invisible in the briefing's data sources — Bayaka and Batwa peoples displaced from forests by logging concessions and conservation parks, whose bushmeat and forest product livelihoods are criminalized while industrial actors extract timber from the same forests.

Enough Project advocacy

Campaigns to end conflict driven by natural resource exploitation in central Africa. Pushes for corporate supply chain transparency, targeted sanctions on conflict mineral networks, and diplomatic engagement on eastern DRC peace processes.

Targets the supply chain chokepoints the briefing identifies — where artisanally mined cobalt and tantalum from eastern DRC enter smelters and refineries, connecting armed group revenues in the Kivus to consumer electronics companies, and advocating for sanctions against the military and political figures who profit.

CODELT (Conseil pour la Défense Environnementale par la Légalité et la Traçabilité) advocacy

Congolese environmental law organization working on forest governance, community land rights, and legal accountability for environmental crimes. Supports local communities challenging logging and mining concessions through DRC's legal system.

Provides legal support to the Congolese communities the briefing identifies as cost-bearers — villages whose forests are allocated as logging concessions without consultation, artisanal miners exposed to toxic conditions in cobalt processing, and fishing communities on rivers contaminated by mining effluent.
