Driven by: Never again—we must build a new Rwanda beyond Hutu and Tutsi

The trap: Transcending ethnicity through a system that depends on ethnic control. The RPF's official ideology eliminates ethnic categories, but the regime is dominated by a Tutsi elite from the anglophone diaspora. Dissent is silenced as 'divisionism.' The unity proclaimed conceals the concentration of power that makes genuine reconciliation impossible. Rwanda's stability is real but brittle—built on silence, not resolution.

For Prayer

Last updated on 2026-07-20.

For those who grow what we drink...

For the smallholder tea and coffee farmers of the highlands and the flower-farm workers of Naivasha — millions of them, most of them women — raising the tea in our cups and the roses on our tables for wages that do not lift them clear of poverty. That the price we pay reaches the hands that pick.

For the drought-struck herders...

For the pastoral peoples of the drylands — Turkana, Maasai, Karamojong — through droughts that kill the herds that are their food and their savings both. For water, for the animals, and for a way of life the fences and the failing rains press in on.

For those who host the fled...

For the great refugee settlements of the region — Kakuma, Bidibidi, Nakivale — where people from South Sudan and Congo and Burundi have waited years, and for the poor communities that carry them. That welcome does not run out.

For the miners...

For the artisanal gold miners of the region, digging for a few dollars a day in unsafe pits, and for the communities beside the industrial mines whose land and water are taken from them.

For the cities' edges...

For the young pouring into Nairobi and Kampala and Dar es Salaam faster than the work can hold them, into settlements without water or title. For dignity in the making of these cities.

For those who organize...

For the cooperatives, the women's savings groups, the labour organizers on the flower farms bargaining for a living wage. That their slow, unglamorous work bears fruit.

Global Witness — Great Lakes investigation

Investigates the 3Ts (tin, tantalum, tungsten) and gold supply chains flowing from artisanal mines in eastern DRC, Rwanda, and Burundi into global electronics markets. Documents how traceability schemes are circumvented and conflict minerals relabeled.

Traces the mineral flows the briefing maps — 3T ores moving from artisanal mines in the Kivus through Rwandan and Ugandan trading houses into international smelters, revealing how due diligence certification can obscure rather than illuminate the armed group revenues and labor exploitation at extraction sites.

Lake Victoria Fisheries Organization (LVFO) investigation

Intergovernmental organization coordinating fisheries management across Kenya, Uganda, and Tanzania's shared Lake Victoria waters. Monitors fish stocks, illegal fishing, and the ecological health of the world's largest tropical lake.

Manages the fishery the briefing identifies as the region's most critical shared resource — where Nile perch overfishing, water hyacinth invasion, and industrial pollution from Kampala and Kisumu converge to threaten the livelihoods of millions of small-scale fishers around the lake's shores.

Fairtrade Africa solidarity

Represents Fairtrade-certified producer organizations across East Africa, including tea and coffee cooperatives in Kenya, Uganda, Rwanda, and Tanzania. Provides market access, premium distribution, and climate adaptation support to smallholder farmers.

International Refugee Rights Initiative (IRRI) advocacy

Researches and advocates on refugee and displacement issues across the Great Lakes region, including Congolese refugees in Uganda, Burundian refugees in Tanzania, and South Sudanese in Kenya. Centers displaced community perspectives in policy.

Amplifies the displaced communities the briefing traces — pastoralists pushed from grazing lands by mining concessions, farming communities displaced by armed group violence in the Kivus, and climate migrants from drought-hit pastoral areas of northern Kenya whose movement is shaped by the same resource pressures the briefing maps.

Natural Resource Governance Institute (NRGI) — East Africa advocacy

Works on extractive sector transparency in Uganda, Tanzania, Kenya, and Mozambique. Monitors oil development in Uganda's Albertine Graben, Tanzania's gas reserves, and Kenya's Turkana oil, pushing for revenue transparency and community benefit sharing.
