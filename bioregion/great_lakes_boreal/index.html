What to bring with you from this briefing

Neskantaga First Nation in northern Ontario has been under a boil-water advisory since 1995 ‚Äî the longest in Canadian history. The federal government promised to end all long-term drinking water advisories on First Nations reserves by 2021. The deadline has been extended multiple times. The current tracker is maintained by the Assembly of First Nations (afn.ca) and updated monthly. Twenty-eight years is not a gap in service. It is a policy position.
Enbridge's Line 5 pipeline carries 540,000 barrels of crude oil daily through the Straits of Mackinac ‚Äî the narrowest point connecting Lake Michigan and Lake Huron. Michigan revoked the pipeline's easement in 2020. The pipeline is still operating while litigation continues. Enbridge's annual report is public. The National Wildlife Federation (nwf.org) maintains a Line 5 risk assessment. A spill at the Straits would be, in the Coast Guard's own assessment, uncontainable by current response capacity.
The Grassy Narrows First Nation has maintained a blockade against clear-cut logging in the Whiskey Jack Forest since 2002. Mercury from the Dryden paper mill has been in their water since the 1960s. The community's health study, conducted with the University of Ottawa, documented elevated rates of neurological damage across three generations. The mercury is still in the riverbed. The remediation plan announced in 2020 has not been completed. FreegrassyNarrows.com documents the ongoing situation.
