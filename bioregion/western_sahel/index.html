Lives Lived Here

On the shores of Lake Chad, Buduma fishermen launch pirogues into water that covers a fraction of what it did sixty years ago. The lake shrank 90% between the 1960s and 1990s and has since stabilised at reduced levels, but the communities who depend on it — approximately 50 million people across four countries — still organise their year around its seasonal flood pulse. As the water recedes from November to February, Kanuri farmers plant recession crops in the exposed lakebed: beans, maize, peppers. Pastoral households water their livestock at the retreating margins. The rhythm holds, but the margin is thinner than it was.
