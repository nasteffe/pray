Lives Lived Here

Before dawn in County Cork, a dairy farmer walks to the parlour to begin the first milking. Her herd of 120 Friesians graze year-round on grass that grows because the Gulf Stream keeps this coast mild — the same latitude as Labrador, but green in January. The milk goes to the Glanbia tanker at the gate, then to the processing plant in Dungarvan, then as infant formula to China and as cheddar to the UK — a supply chain built on the assumption that Irish grass will always grow. The nitrogen she spreads to boost that grass runs into the Blackwater River, where salmon numbers have halved in twenty years. She knows this. The fertiliser bill and the fish kill are both real, and they come from the same field.

For the gifts of this place…

For the gifts of these islands and this coast — for green fields and old harbours, for law and learning long in the making, for the grey sea's long bounty. That plenty may not become forgetfulness.

For what our comfort costs elsewhere…

For the wealth that moves through this place unseen — the money routed through our banks and our islands' quiet arrangements, draining the treasuries of countries that can least afford the loss. Forgive the ease of not asking. Teach us to trace what we hold back to the people who bear its cost.

For those who bear the cost here…

For the emptied fishing towns like Grimsby, where the work went and the pride with it; and for the new poor of wealthy countries, queuing at food banks in the shadow of great fortunes. That prosperity remembers its own left behind.

For quietness…

This week, this place asks of us not alarm but attention. For the discipline to pray without inventing an emergency; for gratitude that does not curdle into complacency; and for love that looks outward from a safe place toward the places that are not safe.

Orano (formerly AREVA) has mined uranium in Arlit, Niger since the 1970s, supplying France's 56 nuclear reactors. The Arlit water table has dropped 75 metres. Tuareg communities report elevated cancer rates. Aghirin'man, a local civil society organisation, has documented these impacts for over a decade. Orano is listed on Euronext Paris. The Trident Trust Company, registered in the British Virgin Islands (a UK Crown Dependency), was identified in the Pandora Papers as managing shell companies used to move wealth from extractive economies into London property. The UK Economic Crime and Corporate Transparency Act 2023 began to address this, but enforcement remains years behind the legislation. Transparency International UK publishes an annual assessment of UK anti-money laundering enforcement that documents the gap between law and practice.
Ireland's dairy herd has grown 40% since the abolition of EU milk quotas in 2015. Nitrogen runoff from that expansion is documented by the EPA in its annual Water Quality in Ireland report — the most recent showing continued decline in river quality, with agriculture as the primary pressure. The salmon in the Barrow, Suir, and Nore are the indicator species. When the salmon are gone, the river has been sacrificed for milk powder exports. The EPA reports are public. The connection between the milk in your supermarket and the fish kill in the Barrow is documented, available, and largely unread.
