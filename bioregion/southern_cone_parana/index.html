Ecosystem

The continent's breadbasket — cerrado and pampas produce soy, beef, and grain for global export, while depending on Amazon-origin rainfall that deforestation threatens

moderate Precipitation 26% of normal; temperatures +0.9°C above baseline; drought conditions in Brazil.

The Paraná runs 3,400 kilometres from the cerrado highlands to the Río de la Plata, and for most of that distance it carries grain. Barges loaded with soy and maize move south along the same river that artisanal fishermen in Corrientes and Entre Ríos have worked for generations — pulling dorado and surubí from currents that grow shallower each decade.

For the gifts of this place…

For the gifts of this open country — the pampas and the great river, the grain and the herds, the generous cities of the south. That plenty may not become forgetfulness.

For what our comfort costs…

For the soy and the beef raised here to feed the world's tables and its livestock — and for the Chaco and Cerrado forests falling to make room, the ground and water spent on a harvest that mostly leaves. Teach us to trace the feedlot and the supermarket back to the cleared frontier.

For those who bear the cost here…

For the Mapuche whose land sits over the Vaca Muerta shale, the Kolla and Quechua beside the lithium flats, and the smallholders pushed to the edges by the advancing soy; and for the poor of the great cities through Argentina's long cycles of crisis. That the country's plenty learns to reach its own.

For holding both…

For honesty about an abundance that clears forests and forgets its first peoples; and for love that reaches from the full granary toward those it passed by.

Carry This

What to bring with you from this briefing

The soy in processed food, animal feed, and cooking oil is likely from the Southern Cone — Argentina and Brazil together produce more than half the world's supply. Bunge, ADM, Cargill, and JBS control the commodity chains. The Roundtable on Responsible Soy (RTRS) certifies a fraction of total production. Ask where the soy in your food chain originates: supermarkets and food manufacturers can trace it if pressed. The EU Deforestation Regulation (EUDR), entering enforcement in 2025, requires supply chain due diligence for soy imported into Europe.
The lithium in your phone, laptop, or electric vehicle battery may come from the Argentine Puna — Salta, Jujuy, or Catamarca provinces — where Arcadium Lithium (formerly Livent, acquired by Rio Tinto 2025) and Albemarle (USA) extract brine from salt flats that Kolla and Atacameño communities depend on for water. Ask your device or car manufacturer for supply chain disclosure on lithium sourcing. ILO Convention 169 requires free, prior, and informed consent from indigenous communities before extraction on their territory — a standard that provincial concessions in Argentina have not consistently met.
The farmed salmon sold in supermarkets worldwide is largely raised in Norwegian-origin operations in Chile's Patagonian fjords — Mowi, Cermaq, AquaChile. Chile uses more antibiotics per tonne of farmed fish than any other country. The Aquaculture Stewardship Council (ASC) certifies some operations. Consumers can ask retailers whether their salmon carries ASC certification and what the antibiotic disclosure policy is for their supply chain.
